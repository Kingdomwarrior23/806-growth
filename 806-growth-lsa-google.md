### 2. What Google Local Service Ads Actually Are

LSA is a *separate* Google ad product from regular Google Ads (pay‑per‑click search ads). Key differences every client needs to understand:

- **LSA is pay‑per‑LEAD, not pay‑per‑click.**  
  The business is only charged when a real customer contacts them (phone call, text, message, or booking).

- **LSA shows at the very top of Google search results**, above even the regular Google Ads, with a "Google Verified," "Google Guaranteed," or "Google Screened" badge.

- **LSA is only available to verified local service businesses**  
  (HVAC, plumbers, roofers, pest control, electricians, garage doors, locksmiths, auto services, cleaning, etc.). Google runs background checks, license checks, and insurance verification.

- **Setup is much simpler than Google Ads.**  
  No keyword research, no ad copywriting, no complex campaign structure. You give Google business info, categories, service area, hours, and budget. Google does the targeting and ad rendering.

- **Risk is partially shifted to Google.**  
  If Google sends a clearly bad or spam lead, the business can dispute it and sometimes get credited back.

**Why this matters for 806 Growth:**  
This is one of the *easiest* paid traffic channels for a new agency to offer, because Google does the heavy lifting on targeting and ad serving. The real leverage is on the back end: answering, following up, disputing bad leads, and growing reviews so the ads produce booked jobs.

### 3. The Hidden Rules Google Uses (Education Gold)

These are facts most business owners do **not** know. They are also some of the most persuasive talking points for marketing, sales calls, and client education.

#### 3a. Google charges for voicemails

If a customer calls from an LSA ad and leaves a voicemail, that can still count as a valid lead and the business can be charged.

Voicemail is no longer "free" — it can be a paid lead that probably will not convert. That is money in the trash.

#### 3b. Google charges for AI receptionist interactions too

Google explicitly allows leads to be counted when a customer "uses an automated system to meaningfully engage" — for example:

- They schedule an appointment  
- They request a callback  
- They provide service details through an AI agent

Google is fine with AI answering. In many ways, Google prefers it over voicemail because it provides a better customer experience.

#### 3c. Missed calls suppress ad visibility

Google tracks the business's **answer rate**. If the business consistently lets calls go to voicemail or misses them entirely, Google will **suppress their impressions**. Even with a high budget, ads simply show less often.

Clients usually assume "budget = visibility." LSA breaks that assumption. Poor responsiveness and missed calls can crush visibility even when spending is available.

#### 3d. Response time is shown publicly on the ad

LSA listings can show "Typically replies in X minutes/hours" right on the ad. When a customer compares three businesses side by side, they often pick the one that replies the fastest.

With an AI text agent, a business can realistically show something like "Typically replies in under 1 minute," which is extremely powerful.

#### 3e. Review velocity beats review quantity

Google weighs **recent reviews** heavily. A smaller business getting five fresh reviews per week can outrank a bigger competitor with hundreds of old reviews.

This is why automated, consistent review requests matter so much: Google wants to see fresh social proof, not just a big legacy number.

#### 3f. Not all leads get credited back

Even with disputes, some leads will not be refunded, for example:

- A valid lead that called after hours and left a voicemail  
- A lead that is valid but never calls the business back

The only way to minimize waste here is to answer 24/7 with AI and reply instantly via text.

**Translation for sales:**  
Voicemail + slow reply + stale reviews = burning money and losing ranking.  
Fix all three = more booked jobs from fewer leads.

### 5. Conversation Flow Framework (4 Phases)

This should be standard in every 806 Growth AI receptionist prompt.

#### Phase 1 — Opening

Goals:

- Greet clearly  
- Identify the business and that it's a virtual assistant  
- Invite them to explain their problem

Pattern:

> "Hi, you've reached [Name], virtual assistant for [Business]. How can I help you today?"

Notes:

- Do **not** pretend it's a human.  
- If someone insists on a live person, the agent should transfer or trigger a callback.

#### Phase 2 — Discovery & Qualification

Goals:

- Understand why they're calling  
- Ask logical follow‑up questions  
- Avoid asking for name/number too early

Example (garage door):

- Does the remote work?  
- Does the wall button work?  
- Do you hear any noise when you press it?  
- Is this urgent today or can it wait?

Principles:

- Make the caller feel understood  
- Use empathetic language  
- Avoid robotic interrogation

#### Phase 3 — Contact & Details Capture

Once the caller feels heard:

- Confirm callback number  
- Ask for name (with spelling)  
- Ask for address or general area if needed

Pattern:

> "Is the number you're calling from a good callback number?"  
> "What's your first name? Can you spell that for me?"

Rule:

> Never open the call with "Can I get your name and number?" That feels like a form, not help.

#### Phase 4 — Closeout and Next Steps

Goals:

- Summarize the situation  
- Tell them exactly what will happen next  
- Reassure them

Pattern:

- Summary: "It sounds like [short restatement]."  
- Next steps: "I'm going to send this to [Name/Role]. You'll hear from them within [time frame]."  
- Reassurance: "We'll get your [door/AC/etc.] working smoothly again."

Result: Caller hangs up feeling taken care of, not left in limbo.

### 10. Deployment: Numbers and Conditional Call Forwarding

Standard deployment pattern:

1. Assign the AI its own phone number in the platform.  
2. Client keeps their existing business number.  
3. Set up **conditional call forwarding** with the client's carrier:
   - If main number rings X times without answer, forward to AI number.  
   - This works for both LSA calls and normal calls that route through the main line.  
4. Configure AI to answer immediately when the forwarded call hits (no extra ringing).

Why this works so well:

- Humans still answer when they can  
- When they don't, **voicemail is bypassed**  
- Every missed call goes to AI, not to a dead end  
- Google sees high answer rates and consistent handling

Alternative setup:

- If the client uses the platform number as their primary number with a mobile app, you can let it ring on the app first, then have the AI catch unanswered calls.

Key principle:

> There should always be *someone* or *something* answering quickly.

## Video X — Local Service Ads vs Google PPC & LSA Setup Details

This video explains the practical differences between LSA and traditional Google PPC, and walks through a full LSA setup and management workflow.

### 1. SERP Layout: Where LSA Fits

For local searches (e.g., "plumber near me"), Google typically shows:

1. **Local Service Ads (Google Guaranteed / Screened)** — pay‑per‑lead  
2. **Search Ads (PPC text ads)** — pay‑per‑click  
3. **Google Map Pack (Business Profiles)** — sometimes with sponsored listings  
4. **Organic results**

Key point: LSA sits at the **very top**, above PPC and Maps, when available.

LSA cards usually show:

- Business name  
- Rating and review count  
- "Google Guaranteed/Screened" badge  
- Location  
- Hours  
- "Call" or "Message" buttons

---

### 2. LSA vs Traditional Google PPC (Economic Difference)

**Local Service Ads (LSA)**:

- Pay‑per‑lead model  
- Businesses are charged when users perform a lead action:
  - Calling via ad  
  - Requesting a message/contact  
  - Sending a booking request  
- Viewing the profile or clicking into the card doesn't itself bill the advertiser  
- Lead prices vary by:
  - Industry (plumber vs tutor vs lawyer)  
  - Geography (big metro vs smaller town)  
  - Service type

**Traditional PPC (search ads)**:

- Pay‑per‑click model  
- Each click costs, regardless of whether the user:
  - Calls  
  - Fills out a form  
  - Leaves immediately

Practical takeaway for clients:

> LSA is often cheaper per *actual lead* than poorly optimized PPC because you are billed on the lead outcome instead of every click.

### 3. LSA Eligibility and Niches

LSA is **not** open to every business type. It is limited to supported categories, which commonly include:

- Home services (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, roofing, garage doors, landscaping, pest, cleaning, etc.)  
- Professional services (law, accounting, some financial services)  
- Real estate  
- Education and tutoring  
- Dental and some healthcare categories  
- Automotive services  
- Beauty and personal care  
- Pet grooming and certain pet services

For 806 Growth, this means a wide surface area across West Texas, but not every possible niche.

**Quick eligibility check method:**

- Search "[your service] near me" in Google  
- If at the top you see cards with a badge and "Call"/"Message," that category is LSA‑enabled in that region

### 7. Lead Management, Response Behavior, and Ranking

The **Leads** tab in LSA functions as a simple CRM.

**Lead statuses and charge states:**

- Not charged yet (pending evaluation)  
- Charged  
- Credited (refunded after dispute or system evaluation)

Google uses call recordings, transcripts, and messages to decide if leads are valid or spam.

**Disputing leads:**

- If a lead is clearly spam, irrelevant, or a job seeker, you can mark and dispute it  
- Valid leads that simply didn't close are not refunded

**Lead tagging and pipeline movement:**

- You can mark leads as:
  - New  
  - Active  
  - Booked  
  - Archived  
- Best practice:
  - Don't leave leads in "new" or "active" for months  
  - Regularly mark them as "booked" when scheduled, and archive when done

Why it matters:

> Google can infer from this behavior whether you actually follow up and convert leads. Clean, consistent tagging shows you're engaged, which supports better LSA performance over time.

**Combined impact:**

- Response time + clean tagging + disputes = Google sees that:
  - You respond fast  
  - You use leads properly  
  - You care about quality

This supports more consistent impressions and stable lead flow, especially when combined with high reviews.

### 8. Running LSA and PPC Together

Strategic recommendation from the video:

- Run **both LSA and PPC** where possible.

Reasons:

- Some weeks LSA dominates; other weeks PPC does  
- LSA covers the very top; PPC takes the next ad positions  
- Combined, the business can "own" more of the first screen

For 806 Growth:

- This fits a progression:
  1. Fix lead handling and reviews (Local Leads Engine)  
  2. Turn on or optimize LSA (Local Service Ads Engine)  
  3. Add PPC search campaigns for extra coverage and volume
